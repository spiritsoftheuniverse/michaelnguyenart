Hello there. I am Michael Nguyen, an artist of the yesteryears. I was born in 1982. That would be nearly two decades before the internet found its way into households. I went to art school for traditional arts; experimenting with mediums like an illiterate chemist. A lot of time was spent onward doing digital illustration. Due to the nature of things, I had to learn all sorts of stuff from within and beyond the arts in order to function creatively. Lots of processes were whittled out through the years, although what remains today are visual arts, 3d modeling/animation, audio engineering, music production, and game development.

On my downtime, I enjoy uniquely crafted video games from a time where hidden techniques were a thing. I also love playing guitar, as it is a rhythmic activity which requires excessive amounts of technical ability depending on your temperament. All things find their way into the spiral of meaning, no matter how big or small. Get that mind right and come correct with those manners the best you can. Nothing makes it any easier, not even the internet.

Join the cozy Discord server to hang out and progress together.

Subscribe to the Youtube channel to see godlike gameplay.

I fell in love with the making of music because I hung out with more musicians than I did artists. Time is of the essence, and there isn't any activity more fluid for the cause than music. My instrument of choice is guitar, played over compositions produced with audio samples to simulate the presence of the rest of the band. I like the genres of origin as inspiration, although what I play are bastardized versions of them all. Since I have been so into creating music, I have never really had the opportunity to enjoy doing cover songs. I can't even remember my own notes, so how can I remember anyone elses? At the point of writing this, music creation has been a part of my life for half of my years of existence.

Kill is a 2.5d side scrolling action video game which commemorates the artistry of legacy video games while moving forward with graphical technology. It ignores modern commercial takes on game design and functionality. There are no graphical reduction options, as its intent is to bring full artistic fidelity to the screen. The world is designed to be a playground of infinite possibilities. Although you start somewhere, it has no ending. As the player of Kill, you take on a creative approach to playing. The reward is the journey.

Kill is entirely non-narrative and all gameplay. The world is inspired by Super Metroid and Castlevania: Symphony of the Night. The combat is inspired by classic fighting games with extreme technical finesse such as Marvel Super Heroes and X-men vs. Street Fighter. It is not only a matter of enemy resistance or challenging obstacles. Much of the challenge is being able to execute your actions well enough to open up all the fun in the game, which are the juggle mechanics and limitless transitions of actions. It is made for technical players who love to challenge themselves to no end through self discovery and experimentation. It gives you everything to be a performance artist of the future.

The best way to follow the development of Kill is to join my Discord server, Michael's Studio.

Video-games are incredibly important to me and are more than just something that I do for fun. Pockets of time represent games at their strongest, so there is no era of gaming that takes all. On the other hand, video games of today are not strong in their ability to provide the player with secret techniques that are only discoverable if you pay attention to your own actions. This is the kind of game that I want more games to be, but the industry has shifted to a fully transparent and boring paradigm of mechanics.

I celebrate the beauty and mechanical finesse of classic games by continuing to play them. The most astonishing titles of all will come to a surprise to most, as these games are not revered by much people at all. These titles include:

  • X-men vs. Street Fighter
  • Castlevania : Dawn of Sorrow
  • Super Mario World

Being the hidden gems of gaming, there is no audience waiting to watch. Then again, there is no audience for technical gameplay. People don't understand what they never discovered. However, if you stick around long enough to know me, you'll know what it means to move like a butterfly and sting like a bee. I still play some games that don't have hidden techniques, such as modern Mario and some pretty strong 3rd person action games, but that's the extent of it. The honorable mentions are as follows:

  • Super Mario 3d World
  • Stranger of Paradise : Final Fantasy Origin
  • Gundam Breaker 3
  • Final Fantasy XV
  • Senran Kagura - Burst Re:Newal

The single player experience offers limitless expression through power-fantasy character dominance, although I really value the friendships made from the classic fighting game community where we found one another. We expect technical excellence through gameplay, something that is very rare. None of the groups I've fleetingly been a part of are remotely concerned about such things. This is why my presence is not a present for them. But in the case of the Online Arcade Discord server, we gift one another with ass whoopings, all with the utmost grace and finesse. I love to death how so many of them are better than me at all sorts of things. That mentality equals growth, and in turn, catch-up to them is entirely possible if you put your mind and heart into it. When I show myself to people who are begrudging of the gift, it's too uncomfortable to bear, and so I bail. Luckily, the fighting game community is a wonderful place.

My youtube channel of course is where you'll find all progression of my gameplay alongside guest appearances with my friends and fighters from all around the world. The channel is generally not for gaming discussion, as I find it appalling that playing is the least important aspect for viewership in today's world of video game content production. I'm here to create performance art, not inundate with content in hopes of garnering subscriptions. The gift of art exists in places you wouldn't expect until someone lays it on you like this.
